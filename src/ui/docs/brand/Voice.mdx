# Voice & Microcopy

The voice is **evocative but unfussy**. It invites you into a story without
sounding like a fantasy-novel cover or a startup landing page. Two registers
coexist:

- **Product / UI voice (chrome):** warm, second-person, direct. Short. Confident. — *"Step into the story." "Pick who you'll become."*
- **In-world voice (narrative):** the roleplay text itself — characters speak in quotes; narration is in *italics*. The product sets the stage; it never writes the story *for* you.

## Rules of thumb

| Aspect | Convention | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Person | Second person ("you"), present tense | *"You arrive at the tavern as the rain begins."* |
| Headlines | Sentence-evocative, often imperative | "Worlds that talk back." |
| Casing | **Sentence case** everywhere. No Title Case headers. | "Continue your scene" — not "Continue Your Scene" |
| Eyebrows / labels | UPPERCASE, tracked, sparingly | "FEATURED WORLDS" |
| Tone | Inviting, a little mysterious, never childish | "A quiet inn. A stranger. A story only you can finish." |
| Length | Short in UI; let the *narrative* be long | Buttons are 1–3 words |
| Emoji | **Never** in UI chrome or marketing | — |
| Punctuation | Em-dashes for atmosphere; ellipses sparingly | "She looks up — and recognizes you." |
| Jargon | Avoid AI/ML jargon ("LLM," "prompt," "token") | say "memory," "scene," "character" |

## Microcopy samples

- **Empty chat:** *"The scene is set. Say something to begin."*
- **Send hint:** "Write your reply — dialogue or action."
- **Memory toast:** "Lyra will remember this."
- **New character CTA:** "Conjure a character"
- **Error (gentle):** "The thread slipped. Try sending that again."
- **Paywall (light touch):** "Keep the story going" — never guilt-y.

> The vibe in one line: **a candlelit invitation to play pretend, taken
> seriously.** Mysterious, warm, literate — never twee, never corporate.
