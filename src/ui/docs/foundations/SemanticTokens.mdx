# Semantic Tokens

The [Colors](?path=/docs/design-system-foundations-colors--docs) page shows the
**raw ramps** — ink, parchment, ember, arcane. This page is the layer on top: a
small set of tokens named for their *role*, not their hue. `--color-fg` instead
of `--color-parchment-50`, `--color-surface` instead of `--color-ink-800`.

Reach for the **role token** wherever you can. It makes intent read clearly at
the call site — `--color-line` says *this is a border* in a way `parchment-50 at
12%` never will — and it keeps the palette movable: re-point a role token and
every component that used it shifts at once, no find-and-replace through the app.

Each token is an alias, read **live** from `theme.css`, so the swatches below
resolve to the exact ramp step they currently point at.

## How they map

- **Foreground** rides the parchment ramp. `fg` is primary text and icons;
  `fg-muted` is secondary; `fg-subtle` is genuinely muted; `fg-faint` is for
  placeholders and disabled glyphs. Never reach past `fg-faint` for readable copy.
- **Surfaces** ride the ink ramp and build depth by *lightening*. `surface` is
  the page; `surface-raised` is cards and controls; `surface-overlay` is
  popovers and raised wells; `surface-sunken` is the deepest well and the scrim
  behind modals.
- **Accents** are the meaning-bearing colors. `accent` (ember) is the one hero —
  primary actions, active states, focus — with `accent-hover` a step brighter.
  `accent-ai` (arcane) marks the AI / magic side and is spent sparingly.
- **Lines** are parchment at low alpha. `line` is the default hairline;
  `line-faint` is the quietest divider; `line-strong` is an emphasised edge. An
  `accent` border is reserved for the active / selected state.

> Rule of thumb: build components against the role tokens. Drop to a raw ramp
> step only when you need a specific shade the role layer doesn't name.
